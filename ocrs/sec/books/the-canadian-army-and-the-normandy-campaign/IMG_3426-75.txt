What has often been forgotten about Montgomery in the ink-spilling con-
troversies that have raged round his name and memory is just how great a
battlefield commander he really was; to U.S. soldier-historian D'Este "he
was certainly a far greater commander than most Americans were willing
to admit or to comprehend. Even his critics have readily acknowledged
his extraordinary organizational brilliance and outstanding professional skill
in the management of battle. In the purely tactical realm he had few equals.
