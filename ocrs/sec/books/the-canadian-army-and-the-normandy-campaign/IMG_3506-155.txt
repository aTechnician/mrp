Apparently in imitation of German practice it was called "Halpenny
Force" after its commander, Lieutenant-Colonel Bill Halpenny. Apart from
one distinguished troop action, however, "Halpenny Force" made no faster
progress on crossing the start line than it had in closing up to it. Instead of
having the Lake Superiors deal smartly with the small hamlet of Gaumesnil,
which lay south of the bomb line and was originally to have been taken by
the 2nd Division, it waited until the Royal Regiment secured it at 1530
hours. By 1800 hours "Halpenny Force" had only advanced beyond Cin-
theaux, but a kilometer away, at which time two companies of Argyll and
Sutherland Highlanders from the 10ch Brigade seized the village, while
another two pushed south to take Hautmesnil. Shortly after this Halpenny
decided in good doctrinal fashion to leaguer two of his tank squadrons in
a "rear rally"
