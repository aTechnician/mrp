The development of a workable doctrine that unified the land armies and air
forces was years in the making. Each campaign taught lessons. Through improve-
ments in communication equipment, the response time from target identification to
aerial engagement was reduced to as little as fifteen minutes. When wireless radios
falled, junior officers or men, seeing friendly Typhoons or Spitfires circling overhead,
waved their arms, got the pilots' attention, and, through exaggerated hand signals,
