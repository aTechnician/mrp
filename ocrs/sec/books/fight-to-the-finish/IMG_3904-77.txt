Much of the German high command hesitated in committing its reserves to
Normandy, for fear of another Allied landing in Calais, but Major-General Fritz
Witt, commander of the 12th SS, recognized the perilous nature of the invasion and
within a few hours of the beach landings, his first units had set off for Caen. Led by
thirty-four-year-old Colonel Kurt Meyer's 25th Panzer Grenadier Regiment, a three-
battalion formation of mechanized infantry, they arrived in Caen early on June 7.
