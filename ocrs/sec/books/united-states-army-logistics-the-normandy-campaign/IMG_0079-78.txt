The railroads also moved cargo to the troops. The chief of transportation
proposed on September 18 that the Red Ball route terminate east of Paris, where
cargoes could be transferred to rail cars.
He argued that since the Military
Railway Service had ample rolling stock to carry 2,000 tons to each army per
day, transferring supplies to rail cars would free up trucks for other duties.
