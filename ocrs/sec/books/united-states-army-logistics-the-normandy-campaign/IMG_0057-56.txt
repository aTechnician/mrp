Disputes arose between combat units and corps headquarters regarding the
expenditure of ammunition.
A case involving the Second Infantry Division
illustrates the problems encountered.
The V Corps ordnance officer informed
the Second Infantry Division that its three attached 4.2-inch mortar companies
had fired 2,527 rounds over their allocation from July 8 to July 13. When the
division G-4 cited authorization from the General Bradley to fire one U/F on
July 11, V Corps Headquarters asked that a copy be sent to the corps ordnance
officer. When Second Division got the situation cleared up on July 15, it
turned out that the July 11 expenditure of ammunition had been authorized,
since the division had built up a credit balance of mortar shells by not expending
their daily allotment. Even with the July 11 expenditure, the division still had
a 1,268-round credit balance as of July 13.50
Therefore, the division had
actually fired less then their allotment for the period, mot more as corps
headquarters had charged.
