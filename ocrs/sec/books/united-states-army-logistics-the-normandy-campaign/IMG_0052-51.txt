While the individuals responsible for the transportation and distribution of
supplies had their difficulties, the "end users," the combat forces, saw logistical
flow in quite different terms.
While the Engineer Special Brigades moved
thousands of tons of supplies, the combat units dealt with supply shortages in
terms of individual items, sometimes in literal life and death terms measuring
the success or failure of the supply system by whether or not they could get the
one hundred Browning Automatic Rifles (BARs) or 1,000 mortar shells
desperately required.
