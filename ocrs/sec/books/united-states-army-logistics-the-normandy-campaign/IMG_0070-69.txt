On the other hand, General Bradley failed for three reasons. First, he
overestimated the ability of the German forces in Brittany to threaten the
western American flank.
Second, he never trusted Patton and his tactics.
Third, he underestimated the logistical need for obtaining the use of Quiberon

the Americans dearly in the long run, extending the war into 1945 and ensuring

This assertion raises several questions.
First, if American forces had
concentrated on seizing the ports, how long would it have taken? Given the
tenacity which they held onto the other ports, German defenders would have
probably held out for days, if not weeks, and battle damage and demolition
might have been extensive. Second, how long would it have taken to put them
into operation considering Germany's ability to destroy facilities? Third, even
if the engineers put them into service in a timely manner, how quickly could the
railroads have been restored?
Did the Americans have the transportation
capacity to move the supplies all the way to Patton's forces from Brittany?
How does the performance of the Red Ball Express truck route fit into Colonel
Mack's calculations?
Opening ports earlier did not increase the number of
trucks available to move supplies forward. These questions that lie at the heart

The combining of ETOUSA general and special staffs with the
COMZ general and special staffs meant there was no organization to adjudicate
differences between the COMZ and the field and air forces over supply

forces, believed such adjudication was necessary because COMZ, in its capacity
as the ETOUSA staff, gave it excessive powers and an unfair advantage. Not
surprisingly, Lee wrote a dissenting letter for inclusion in the report.
