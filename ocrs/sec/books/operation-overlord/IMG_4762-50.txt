Chapter 5
A HOPE THAT FAILED
The Russian counteroffensive in the winter of 1942-43,
which began at Stalingrad with the encirclement and virtual
annihilation of the Sixth German Army, was expected by
Allied military authorities to have serious repercussions on
both the military and domestic situation in Germany. Follow-
ing close upon the heels of the British victory at El Alamein
in October 1942 and the Anglo-American invasion of north
Africa in November, German
Allied strategists
thought, would reach the breaking point. Much in the nature
of a German demoralization was expected from these events
and from strategic bombing.
The "Rankin" Plan for Collapse of Germany
The machinery was therefore set in motion in the spring
of 1943, despite the intensive planning that was going on
for "Overlord," to prepare plans for an Allied landing
on
the European continent under conditions created by the in-
ternal weakening and
disintegration of Germany. Such
landing was considered likely to be possible not later than
the winter of 1943-1944, and probably earlier. The Com-
bined Chiefs of Staff decided to prepare for and take advan-
tage of such an eventuality and instructed COSSAC to draw
up plans for a return to the Continent under conditions of a
voluntary German withdrawal from France or a total breakup
of the German Army. The code name "Rankin" was selected
