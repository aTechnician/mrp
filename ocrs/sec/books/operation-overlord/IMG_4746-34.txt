Determining Soil Trafficability on the Beaches
Since the "Overlord" invasion was planned to take place
over beaches, the solidity of the soil had to be determined
to find out whether heavy trucks, tanks, bulldozers, artillery
pieces, and other mechanized equipment could pass over
them. Geological surveys were therefore made to determine
the nature of the soil on the Normandy beaches. Daring night
raids were carried out by specially-trained and specially-
equipped units of Combined Operations Command and the
Royal Navy, bringing back samples of the subsoil. So as not
to arouse German suspicions as to the particular stretch of
coast in which the Western Allies were interested, raids were
carried out far from the Normandy area. 45 The condition of
the sea bed near the invasion coast had also to be determined
if the artificial harbors were to be sunk in the correct depth
of water, or, in the first place, if they were to be correctly
designed.
