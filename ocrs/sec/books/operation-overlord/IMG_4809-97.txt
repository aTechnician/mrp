Regrouping of First U. S. Army and Continued Attack
Despite the small territorial gains made on the eastern
flank of the front, operations proceeded according
to the
strategic plan, which had as its principal aim the pinning
down of the available German reserves on the Second British
Army front, thereby permitting the First United States Army
to take Cherbourg and regroup for a drive to the Loire
against a minimum of opposition. The numerous tank battles
which the Second Army forced the Germans to fight and its
aggressive actions eastward and southward from the bridge-
head on the east bank of the Orne, further confirmed the
Germans in their belief that the main Allied threat lay where
they assumed it logically would.
