THE BEACHHEAD WIDENED
The first phase of "Overlord" merged into the second
imperceptibly. Even as infantry troops fought their way into
Carentan, on all other sectors of the front the battle main-
tained its momentum in an effort to expand the beachhead
into one wide enough and deep enough to permit the con-
centration of large forces for the development of the cam-
paign on a broad front toward Germany. As the American
V Corps advanced south on
Caumont, just north of the
Caen-Avranches highway, and the British I and XXX Corps
engaged the German armor in the area of Tilly-sur-Seulles,
the American VII Corps was
beginning operations to cut
the Cotentin Peninsula and capture the port of Cherbourg.
