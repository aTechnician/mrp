FOREWORD
"Overlord" was unquestionably, as of this writing,
largest overseas military operation ever undertaken. In the
pages which follow, Dr. Albert Norman presents, insofar as
it can be compressed within one easily readable volume, a
careful history of the planning which made its achievement
possible and of the operation itself.
Dr. Norman's topic is absorbing, both for its historical
interest and for the lessons it holds for those who, perhaps
concerned with the possibility of
"Overlords" yet to come. It holds yet another and even more
important interest. The staff groups which contributed to the
success of "Overlord" and the ultimate defeat of Germany
were the exemplification of an idea of allied unity, developed
by General Eisenhower and perfected to such an extent that
