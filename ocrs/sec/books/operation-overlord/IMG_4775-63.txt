Assessing German Strategy
In the matter of assessing German strategic moves, once
"Overlord" was begun, the general conclusion was reached
by the higher planning stafts that the pattern of German
strategy would be based on inflicting an early and decisive
defeat upon the "Neptune" invasion forces so that the initia-
tive could be regained once more on the Russian front. For
that reason it was held that the Germans would retain mini-
mum defensive forces on the Eastern front, in the Balkans,
and in Italy in order to build up sufficient forces in France
with which to defeat the Western Allies in Normandy. The
Germans, it was thought, would almost certainly take grave
risks elsewhere in order to defeat "Overlord. Â»42 In no case,
however, was it considered possible for the Germans, in view
of their commitments
elsewhere, to transfer to the north
of France more than eight divisions from other fronts.
