A stratagem, designed to deceive the Germans into believing
that the Normandy landings, when they took place, were
merely a diversion from the main invasion assault that would
come some weeks later against the Pas-de-Calais coast, was
given consideration. It was hoped that the Germans would
react to the deception and retain their strong defensive gar-
rison in the Pas-de-Calais intact and not rush troops into
the Normandy battle area. The success of the stratagem was
