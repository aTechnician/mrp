an ocean-going ship that could land armored units on enemy
beaches. Experiments were begun with oil tankers employed
on Lake Maracaibo, in Venezuela. Because of their shallow
draft, these tankers were considered the most suitable for the
purpose. They had their bows cut off and a ramp fitted.
After successful experiments they were placed in mass pro-
duction both in Britain and the United States. In general,
invasion vessels fell into two main types: The larger vessels,
such as LST and LCI(L), mostly of British design and
development, were for sea-going purposes, and the
smaller
craft, such as LCM and LVT, largely of American design,
were used for ship-to-shore traffic. 35 The most important of

of operations was the sea-going LST, the production of which
