Air Force and Naval Tasks
Considerable bombing of tactical targets was contemplated
both before and during the landings in France. In the im-
mediate pre-invasion phase, railroads, bridges, radar stations,
coastal batteries, and similar targets were to be bombed.
More than 2,300 light and medium bombers as well as fighter-
bombers were available to the Allied air forces. There were,
besides, about 3,500 heavy bombers and 5,000 fighter planes
ready for the invasion. Close to 1,400 troop carrier planes
and 3,300 gliders were available to the airborne force. 5t
