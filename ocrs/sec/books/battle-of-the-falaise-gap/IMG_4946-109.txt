Colonel MacHugh, according to Field Order No 6 signed by
General MacBride, had the task of "capturing Hill 213, north
of Argentan and, in conjunction with other forces, of taking
Argentan, helping to close the Falaise-Argentan pocket and by
so doing to destroy the whole of the Seventh German Army."
This plan was to be delayed for two days.
Colonel MacHugh had immediately seen the difficulties:
fighting in the ruins controlled by a spur held by armour of the
Iloth Panzer and the infantry of the 728th Infantry Regiment
of the Wehrmacht, and the dense forest rising to 244 feet,
hiding the horizon, a forest which the French had reported as
being heavily defended. "I wanted to flank the wood to the
right, but was not permitted to do so, because I would then
be going into the goth U.S. Division territory. This annoyed me
extremely because there was a gap of three miles between my
unit and troops of the goth Infantry Division."
