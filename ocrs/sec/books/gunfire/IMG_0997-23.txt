No development of a modern heavy piece
took place before the Second World War, despite
the fact that the need for heavy guns had been
distinctly recognised during the Great War 1914-
1918. Only a limited number of formations with
obsolete guns were retained. As mentioned, it was
stated in the 1930s by the RAF, and by some
army officers, that bomber aircraft could take
over the role of heavy artillery in the future, so
there was no need to develop new guns of this
type, it was argued! Hence, 'a dead hand' was laid
over the development programme. Those units,
which were retained despite this attitude, were in
most cases equipped with the 6-inch Mark XIX
and the
8-inch howitzer. The Heavy
Regiments typically had one battery equipped
with the former and two or three with the latter.
