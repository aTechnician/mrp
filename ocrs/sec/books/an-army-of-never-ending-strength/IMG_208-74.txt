Despite heavy infantry losses for the 4th Canadian Armoured Division in the
breakthrough battles to Falaise and the Falaise Pocket battles, operations con-
tinued without a break into late August and September. The division swept
across northern France as part of the 2ist Army Group's pursuit of the retreating
Germans. Advancing towards the Seine as part of the 2nd Canadian Corps, the
division crossed the river at Elbeuf on 26 August. Operating on the right flank
of the 2nd Canadian Infantry Division, it liberated St. Omer on 5 September.

As a result of the reduced German ability to attack Canadian units behind
the front, the army-level artillery units of the 2nd Canadian Army Group Royal
Arillery (AGRA) continued to be highly effective between September 1944 and
May 1945 but with far fewer casualties. During the summer, losses in the AGRA
units had increased significantly because of friendly fire in the way of inaccurate
aerial bombing. These types of operations by Allied four-engined bombers were
largely concluded by September. Total battle casualties for all three medium
regiments in northwest Europe from 13 July to 4 October had been 250. Non-
battle casualties totalled 115." Recorded monthly battle casualties for the re-
mainder of the war for the three medium regiments totalled 190. Within this
regiments, the number of nonbattle casualties that required hospitalization
over the same period (October 1944 to May 1945) was an astounding 960.
