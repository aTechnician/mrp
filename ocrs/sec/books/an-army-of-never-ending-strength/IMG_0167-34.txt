Once an order was received to send tanks forward, the appropriate number
would then be driven by rail or tank transporter from "F" Squadron to the 25th
CADR's corps delivery squadron.? This squadron would then feed the two
forward-delivery squadrons of the 25th CADR. Within their operating bases,
25th CADR squadrons operated "tank circuits" staf at each stop in the circuit
would check over an area of the tank. This repetitive inspection-and-receiving
process at each squadron could take time. While of questionable efficiency, it
was effective for resolving issues with newly arrived vehicles and prevented
these problems from reaching armoured regiments in the field. At any point,
repaired vehicles that had come from the RCEME's facilities could join a 25th
CADR squadron.
To meet demands for new AFVs quickly, 25th CADR squadrons usually had
an AFV float, or balance, that they could draw from; it was similar to the CBRG'S
reinforcement units. This float was the percentage of the total AFVs in service
with the front-line units that a 25th CADR squadron was designated to support.
Though this percentage went up and down with supply and demand, an effort
was made to maintain the desired inventory level to deal with emergencies. This
