FOLLOWING THE NORMANDY CAMPAIGN, for the remainder of the month of
August and into September, the Allies pursued fleeing German forces across
northern France. In the course of the pursuit, perimeters were established
around ports on the English Channel that still held German garrisons. Despite
the euphoria of victory that marked the closure of the Falaise Gap and the
subsequent high speed pursuit, there were still instances of heavy combat.

already struggling with low strength in its infantry companies, this battle was
especially taxing. Luckily for the division, apart from the unopposed liberation
of Dieppe on 1 September and some minor actions near Dunkirk, it saw little
action in early September. The heavily fortiffed port of Dunkirk was originally
designated to be captured by the division, which, by 9 September, had liberated
some of the surrounding towns. As it advanced across France in August and
early September, German channel garrisons were its main opposition. During
this period, the division continued to carry out operations despite an increasing
shortage of infantry, other ranks, replacements. The worst period for the division
lasted from late August to mid-September, when all infantry regiments were
understrength because of losses in the summer. As illustrated in detail in Appen-
