All the above goes quite some way to explaining why there was nearly three months between
the Scheldt Estuary action (9 Oct-13 Nov 44) and Millingen-Leuth action (9-18 Feb 45).
After the Walcheren/Scheldt operations were completed the Assault Squadrons took on a
garrison/supply/civilian rescue role, as the area was mainly flooded. The need to supply the
needs of all within this zone would require the use of amphibious devices to cover some duties
that in the normal course of events would be a civilian function. This led to an increase in the
number of Buffaloes required for this duty and the anticipated advance across Holland as well
as Germany.
During autumn every attempt had been made to bring up both sufficient consumable supplies
and bridging equipment with some thousands of tons of Bailey Bridge components which where
needed to either replace damaged bridges or erect new ones across the mass of waterways.
Squadrons of the 4 and 11 RTR (Royal Tank Regiment) where to change to Buffaloes and join
the other 'Ferrymen'. I say ferrymen; as the accounts I have found in various publications gave
only brief details 'ferried from A to B'. At this point it should be noted also that further
squadrons of ASRE or AES (Armoured Engineers Squadron) as we had become known, also
changes from 5th Assault Regiment to 5 Armoured Engineer Regiment.
