You are likely to stay at the Disembarkation Camp Unit from 12 to 36 hours, the actual
period being dependent on train timings and the size of the draft which is to be handled.
6. From the Military Disembarkation Camp Unit you will proceed to a Military Dispersal Unit.
7. The Military Dispersal Unit to which you will be sent will be the one which serves the
area in which the address you have entered on page I of your Release Book is situated. Your
permanent address should be shown on all other pages if it be different from that shown on
page I. The Railway Warrant on page Io will only be issued for a journey from the Military
Dispersal Unit to the address shown on page I of your Release Book.
8. You are again reminded that as the whole procedure from the time you arrive at your
port of disembarkation to the time you arrive at your home is only approximately 72 hours
you should carry with you the minimum amount of baggage, which should include:
(a) the arms, equipment, etc., which you have to hand in at the Military Disembarkation
Camp Unit;
(b) the essentials you require during the 72 hours of release procedure;
(c) such items as you may require for your own use immediately after release.
Should you become a casualty on the journey, ensure that you obtain your Release Book
from your draft Conducting Officer.
