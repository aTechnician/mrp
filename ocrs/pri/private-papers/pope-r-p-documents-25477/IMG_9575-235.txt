With reference to Allowance Regulations 1938, para 34, the following
orders will be observed: - 1 Troops in Billets or Hutted Camp.
(a) only in exceptional circunstances will RUM be demanded or issued, e. g.
where men have become soaked and have no dry change of clothing, and then only
on the authority of the Commander of the Division, Area, A.A. Bde, or Independent
Bde concerned, supported by the recommendation of their Senior-Medical Officers.
(b) Cocoa, milk and sugar on the scale laid down in para 53, Allowance Regulations
1938 (i.e. for each pint: Cocoa, 1/4 oz;milk (condensed), 4/5 oz; sugar, ½ oz) may be
issued to those troops who have been subjected to hardship or exposure on the
recommendation of the Senior Medical Officer.
2. Troops under Canvas or in Bivouac
(a) RUM may be issued to individuals who have been subjected to severe exposure
if recommended by the Senior Medical Officer, and approved by the Commander of the
Division, Area, A.A. Bde, or Independent Bde concerned.
(b) Cocoa with milk and sugar may be issued under the same conditions as in 1 (b)
above.
3. Method of Rum Issues
If authorised it will be issued only to those individuals named by an Officer
or Warrant Officer, and such Officor or arrant Officer will be held responsible
that issues are not made to individuals who have not ben subjected to the conditions
which gave rise to the issue.
