Chapter Seven
WINTER: THE MAAS RIVER LINE
Discover the soldier
Here by the inundations of the Maas,
At one with nature and at variance
With the elements
That ever since one grey, immortal dawn
On the blazing Calvados coast
Have belaboured constantly
The fighting men.
War has suspended
Even the cultivation of the soil :

The pines are silent,
Deserted the excavations among the dunes,
Trappings and traces of the unnatural Germans
In lonely mist.
Only the quiet nook in the shell-splintered wood
Restores the sweet sense of rest
Where men were gently laid
Beneath the earth.
The wearied sentry
Sees only the devastation of the day
The puppy that survived, and licked his hand
And won his heart
So long deprived of affection: stares at the blackness
Over the evil river ;
Recovering, remembers
Another country.
