The Vehicle Reserve Depots
On the provision side, the flow of vehicles from the Ministry of
Supply contracts was not long in getting under way,
The British
load-carrying vehicles were nearly all of the single driven axle type
during the first year of the war, but as time went on many manu-
facturers built four-wheel drive lorries, and to the ranks of these
came the Bedford lorry firm (General Motors) and the great
numbers made by this firm were of inestimable value.
It was soon
evident that the one vehicle reserve depot at Feltham could not cope
