This chapter and the next one examine the contribution made to the conduct of
the Northwest Europe campaign by the two armies permanently under the
command of the 21st Army Group —the Second (British) and the First Canadian.
Lieutenant-Generals Miles Christopher Dempsey and Henry Duncan Graham
Crerar respectively commanded these formations.
The contribution made at
army level to the overall operational approach of the 21st Army Group was
determined by the following three interconnected aspects: the operational
function of an army; the relationship between army group and army; and the
extent to which these armies implemented or modified army group operational
methods.
This study will now examine each of these aspects with regard to
Dempsey and the Second (British) Army.
For the Normandy campaign, the First United States Army also served under
Montgomery's operational command. However, in reality, Montgomery held on
a very loose sein the commander of the First Army, General Omar N. Bradley.

treat Bradley differently from Crerar and Dempsey. For though Bradley's Army
