Taking The Strain
The railways in France were critical in delivering and sustaining the BEF's deployment including
logistic support, engineering projects, defence works, airfield construction, unit, personnel
and equipment reinforcement, leave, course, training and rotation programmes and medical
evacuation. Without the railways, the BEF, like the French Army, could not have functioned,
which is why they were central to defence planning in Western Europe in 1939 and 1940.
