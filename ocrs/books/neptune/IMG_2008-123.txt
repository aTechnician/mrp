The LCT crewmen were also required to wear special chemically treated
overall that were supposed to protect them from a gas attack. Upon don-
ning them, many sailors wondered if poison gas might not be preferable.
The treated clothing smelled like rotten eggs, and the stench was so pow-
ful that some sailors gagged. "These clothes covered all of our bodies
from the neck down," a sailor recalled, "and they were hot as hell, itchy, and
the odor was unbearable." The treated material did not breathe at all, and
one sailor described it "like walking around in a steam bath all day and in a
suit of ice at night." Even the sailors' traditional white hats were dyed blue,
presumably to make them less visible at sea, though many of the sailors
resented having to sacrifice this particular tradition to some planner's
notion of security.!4
