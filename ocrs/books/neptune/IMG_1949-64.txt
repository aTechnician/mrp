The second issue Morgan had to deal with was how many Allied divi-
sions would be needed. The coast of Normandy could accommodate only
so many men at a time, and that necessarily limited the initial landing to
only a few divisions. The more important question for the planners was
the total number of divisions needed to complete the conquest of occu-
pied France and drive on to Berlin. Back during the Arcadia conference,
Churchill had suggested that such a campaign would require forty divi-
