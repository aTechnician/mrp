7. The enemy may use smoke in conjunction with gas.
He may put small quantities of gas into a smoke screen to
simulate a gas attack and to compel the use of respirators;
or he may use smoke to extend the flanks of a cloud gas
attack, so as to conceal the actual frontage of the cloud and
to induce the wearing of respirators on a wide front. Smoke
shells may often be fired in combination with gas shells.
It is also possible that smoke may be used in conjunction with
a low-flying spray attack.
8. Methods of defence against gas are dealt with in Sec. 40.
