Oil Supply
Oil supply was only to a small extent a military responsibility.
The Army, which was only one, and that not the biggest, of the
claimants on resources, undertook the executive task of moving its
own supplies forward from the ports in overseas theatres; but in this
country the oil companies, grouped in a special organization known
as the Petroleum
Board, were responsible for
distributing
supplies, both civil and service.
Moreover the whole task of pro-
curing supplies from their sources and shipping them to the various
theatres, including the United Kingdom, was performed by the oil
industry, which, however, worked under supervisory Government
committees on which the Army
was represented.
Ultimate
responsibility for all oil questions rested with a sub-committee of
the Cabinet, the Oil Control Board, of which the Quarter-Master-
General was a member.
