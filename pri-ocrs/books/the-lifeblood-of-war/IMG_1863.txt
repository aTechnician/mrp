'Our trains (wagon trains) at this time could not carry supplies of provisions
and short forage for the army, with the necessary ordnance, hospital stores,
camp equippage, etc. for more than six or eight days. A wagon drawn by six
mules over good roads can haul 1,200 short rations of provisions (bread,
sugar, coffee, salt, and soap) and six days of grain for mules. Over hilly or
muddy roads the weight would be correspondingly reduced. It can thus be
easily seen how far from depots an army can be supplied by wagons. When
supplies in trains (wagon trains) become exhausted, an army must be at or
near another source of supply as a matter of course.
