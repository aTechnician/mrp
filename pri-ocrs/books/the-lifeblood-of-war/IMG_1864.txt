For the initial invasion the Luftwaffe deployed 500 combat aircraft and
another 571 Ju 52/3M transports. It was the latter which brought in the six
companies of airborne troops who captured the Norwegian capital with its
250,000 inhabitants; it was Ju 52s again which dropped the 120 paratroops
who captured Stavanger aerodrome shortly before eight o'clock on the
morning of the first day. By that evening, 180 Ju 52s arriving with clock-work
regularity, had touched down in Stavanger. In the course of the whole
campaign (but their chief use was in the early stages) these invaluable aircraft
flew 3,018 sorties, carrying 29,280 personnel, 259,300 imperial gallons of fuel
and 2,376 tons of supplies? Quoting William Green, Warplanes of the Third
