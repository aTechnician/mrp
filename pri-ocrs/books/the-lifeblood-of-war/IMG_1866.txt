19. The British found themselves in the same position on the Western Front in the
period 1914-17, and indeed to a lesser extent until the end of the First World War.
1o begin with, they were so vastly inferior numerically to the French that there was
no question of pursuing a private strategy, they simply lacked the means. From
1917, following the French Army mutinies, and the need for the British to shoulder
the major burden of the offensives on the Western Front, they had more say in
strategy, but were not in any sense able to conduct it in isolation.
