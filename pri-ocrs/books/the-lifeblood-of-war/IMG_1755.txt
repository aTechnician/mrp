A Final Comment and an Unheeded Warning
Seeking a final comment on the logistic aspects of a war fought
against an Asian nation, one could find few more apposite than the
view expressed in the fourth volume of the United States Army Official
History of the Korean War published in 1966:
'Lacking construction equipment, Chiang Kai-shek had used hand labor to
construct the airfields for US planes in World War II and successfully completed
the huge task. In Korea the Chinese again demonstrated how manpower could be
used in quantity to take the place of machines. Although this process might be
uneconomical and wasteful in principle, it was effective as an expedient and as a
countermeasure In this case superior technology, far from leading to an easy
victory, produced no victory at
all. It would be unfortunate if the hard-won
lessons learned in the Korean War, both on the battlefield and in the negotiations
should be ignored or forgotten because of the absence of victory?30
By 1966 the United States would again be pitting itself against an
Asian nation.
