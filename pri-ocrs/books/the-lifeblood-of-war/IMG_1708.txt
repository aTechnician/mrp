It took weeks, and sometimes months, to stock up for a big offensive.
The huge piles of shells and bombs were difficult to hide and could be
seen on air photographs, signalling the intention of the commander to
the enemy. Once ammunition dumps had been established, the effort
required to move them was equally prodigious. Changing the sector in
which a major push was to be mounted, meant a postponement while
the move which, in its turn, would be seen by the enemy air
reconnaissance, took place.
